# Stay Safe Online: Checklist for College Students in India

A practical guide to protect yourself from cyber threats, scams, and online fraud.  

---

## 1. General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for each account and update them regularly.  
2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** for emails, social media, and cloud accounts.  
3. **Avoid clicking suspicious links or attachments** from unknown emails, SMS, or social media.  
4. **Limit sharing personal info** (Aadhaar, PAN, phone number, address) on public platforms.  
5. **Keep devices and apps updated** with the latest security patches.  
6. **Log out from shared/public devices** and avoid saving passwords on them.  

---

## 2. Financial / UPI Safety

7. **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP**—official bank staff or payment apps will never ask for it.  
8. **Verify collect requests and QR codes** before making payments; confirm the recipient’s details carefully.  

---

## 3. Reporting Online Crime

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)  
- **Helpline:** 1930  

> Report phishing, fraud, or financial scams immediately to protect yourself and others.  

---

## REMINDER

Always stay alert online. Protect your accounts, verify unknown requests, and report suspicious activity immediately. Prevention is the best defense against cybercrime.
